About LaunchStatic

We help solo founders and indie hackers get a credible landing page online without learning a framework, configuring a build pipeline, or paying monthly fees for a page builder.

Our mission

Indie hackers lose days — sometimes weeks — on deployment and tooling before they ever talk to a customer. LaunchStatic exists to shrink that gap. You should be able to download an HTML file, change the copy, push it to Cloudflare Pages, and share a link with your first beta users before dinner.

We are not trying to replace your product codebase. We are trying to get your marketing page out of the way so you can focus on what you are actually building.

What we offer

  • Free templates — SaaS, app, and waitlist landing pages under the MIT license. Download, modify, deploy. No account required.
  • Deploy guides — Step-by-step walkthroughs for Cloudflare Pages, GitHub Pages, and Netlify with zero build configuration.
  • Tools and references — Checklists and utilities that help you ship static sites faster.
  • Paid template packs (coming) — Curated multi-page kits for specific niches (developer tools, newsletters, portfolios) with premium layouts and extended sections. The free templates will always stay free.
  • Custom builds — If you need a done-for-you landing page, we take on a limited number of projects. Get in touch with your timeline and budget.

What we are not

LaunchStatic is a template and resource site, not a hosted website builder. We do not host your pages, manage your DNS, or provide a drag-and-drop editor. You download plain HTML files and deploy them wherever you want.

That is intentional. Hosted builders charge recurring fees and make it hard to leave. Static files on Cloudflare Pages cost nothing at low traffic, load fast globally, and belong to you forever. If you outgrow a template, you already have the source — no export button, no migration headache.

Who this is for

LaunchStatic is built for people who ship side projects, validate ideas with waitlists, launch apps on the App Store, and sell SaaS tools from a one-person company. If you have ever abandoned a landing page because Next.js config ate your Saturday, this site is for you.
